01 / Foundation

The four phases of writing

Writing is not one big leap — it's four steps. Every stage in this pathway helps you move from one phase to the next, with the right kind of support at the right time.

①
Plan
Read the task. Brainstorm ideas. Structure your notes (mind map, table).
②
Draft
Use a template. Write paragraphs. Connect ideas with linking words.
③
Revise
Use the checklist. Get feedback. Fix content, structure, vocab and grammar.
④
Present
Display your final text in the Gallery Walk and read what others wrote.
02 / Choose your level

Pick what challenges you — but feels possible.

The three levels match the steps of the Berlin Rahmenlehrplan. Most Year 8 students work in Level I (E/F) or Level II (F/G). If you need more support to get started, choose Starter. You can always switch up or down.

01

Session 1 · Plan & Draft

Step by step, you'll move through the planning and drafting phases. Work through each stage in order — every workspace saves automatically in your browser.

⏱ 60 minutes total
1.1 Read the task carefully ⏱ 5'

Look at your task. Mark or note three things every writing task tells you:

① AudienceWho are you writing to? (formal / informal?)
e.g. a friend in London, a head teacher, blog readers …
② PurposeWhy are you writing? What do you want to do?
e.g. to invite, to inform, to convince, to share an experience …
③ ContentWhich points must your text cover?
List the bullets or aspects from your task.
Tip · Hilfekarte „Dealing with texts" Read the task at least twice. Mark key words with a coloured pen. If a word is new, look it up before you start writing.
1.2 Brainstorm & structure your notes ⏱ 10'

Now collect ideas. Don't write full sentences yet — just words and phrases. Pick one structuring method:

Mind mapTopic in the centre, branches outwards. Best for creative or open topics (story, blog).
★ Topic → branch 1, branch 2, branch 3 …
TableTwo or three columns. Best for comparing ideas (pros/cons, before/after).
| Argument | Example | Explanation |
Herringbone diagram (fish-bone)Main line = topic, side bones = subtopics. Good for reports.
Topic ─┬─ when ─┬─ where ─┬─ what ─┬─ why
Watch out Don't try to write perfect sentences yet. Mistakes here cost you time you'll need later.
1.3 Use your structure template ⏱ 5'

Below is the structure for your chosen text type at your chosen level. Use the left column to remind yourself what each part should contain. Click any sentence starter on the right to copy it, so you can paste it into your draft.

Pick a level and a text type above to see your template.
How to use this Copy a sentence starter, then continue in your own words. The starters are scaffolds — you replace them or extend them with your own ideas.
1.4 Write your first draft ⏱ 35'

Now write. Keep the template open and use your notes from stage 1.2. Don't aim for perfection — aim for a complete draft you can revise next session.

0 words
Tip · paragraphs & topic sentences Start a new paragraph for each new idea. The first sentence of a paragraph (the topic sentence) tells the reader what the paragraph is about.
Tip · linking words Connect ideas: first, then, after that, finally · because, so, therefore, however · for example, in addition, besides.
1.5 End of Session 1 — save & rest ⏱ 5'

Read your draft once, slowly. Don't fix anything yet. Distance helps you see things you can't see now. Save your draft (it stays in your browser) and come back fresh for Session 2.

Before you close the browser Your draft is saved automatically — but copy it into your school cloud or e-mail it to yourself as a backup, just in case.
02

Session 2 · Revise with AI feedback

Today you ask a privacy-safe AI tool (e.g. fobizz, SchulKI) to give you feedback on your draft. Then you implement the feedback and finalise your text.

⏱ 60 minutes total
2.1 Re-read your draft & self-check ⏱ 10'

Read your draft from yesterday. Tick what you've already done well. Don't tick things you're unsure about — leave them empty for now.

Pick a level and a text type above to see your checklist.
Tip · Hilfekarte „Proofreading" Read your text aloud. Read it backwards (from the last sentence up) to catch spelling mistakes. Cover the lines below the one you're reading with a piece of paper.
2.2 Ask the AI for structured feedback ⏱ 10'

Open your school's privacy-safe AI tool (e.g. fobizz Tools, SchulKI, telli) and use the prompt below. The prompt is built around the same checklist criteria you just used — so the AI will check the four areas you need: content, structure, vocabulary, grammar.

Pick your text type and level above to see the matching AI prompt.
Important · privacy Use only the AI tool your school recommends. Don't paste personal data (full names, addresses, phone numbers) into the AI. Replace them with placeholders like [my friend] or [my city].
What the AI gives you back The AI will return feedback in four sections (content / structure / vocabulary / grammar). For each section it tells you what's good and what to improve. Don't accept everything blindly — the AI can be wrong. You decide what to change.
2.3 Implement the feedback ⏱ 30'

Work through the AI's feedback systematically. Tackle big issues first (content, structure), then language (vocabulary, grammar). After each change, re-read the paragraph you've changed.

0 words
Strategy · revise in this order 1) Content — is anything missing? · 2) Structure — paragraphs and linking words · 3) Vocabulary — repetitions, simpler/stronger words · 4) Grammar & spelling — last, with the help of a dictionary.
2.4 Final proofread ⏱ 10'

One last pass before the gallery walk. Use this final mini-checklist:

CONTENT
STRUCTURE
VOCABULARY & SPELLING
GRAMMAR
04 / Showcase

Gallery Walk · share what you wrote

The whole class displays their final texts on the walls (or on tables). For 30 minutes, you walk around, read others' work, and leave kind, useful comments on sticky notes.

How the Gallery Walk works

  1. Print or display your final text. Add your name (or pen-name) on top.
  2. Walk silently for the first 10 minutes — read at least four other texts.
  3. Leave feedback on a sticky note: one thing you liked + one question / suggestion. Use the “Two stars and a wish" format.
  4. Read the comments you got, and pick one piece of feedback you'll remember for next time.
  5. Whole class: share one new word, phrase or idea you discovered today.

Two stars and a wish: ★ I liked … · ★ It works because … · ✦ I wish / I wonder …
